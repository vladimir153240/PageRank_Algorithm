### **2. Methodology: Steps to Comply with Requirements**
To ensure a passing grade, we must adhere to the "scientific method" and the specific logistics defined in the guidelines.

**Phase 1: Setup & Logistics (Crucial)**
*   **Repository Creation:** Create a GitHub repository immediately.
*   **The "10 Commits" Rule:** You **must** make at least 10 meaningful commits on 10 different days.
    *   *Plan:* Do not wait until the end. Commit your initial outline today, your research notes tomorrow, your first function next week, etc.

**Phase 2: Execution & Coding**
*   **Select Format:** Choose between a Technical Report, Tutorial, or Article. A Jupyter Notebook (`.ipynb`) is mandatory.
*   **Math Implementation:** Ensure you define the mathematical basis clearly (formulas, LaTeX) before implementing the Python code.
*   **Code Quality:** Use functions, clear variable names, and comments. Avoid "spaghetti code" inside the notebook cells.

**Phase 3: Documentation & Analysis**
*   **Problem Formulation:** Clearly state *what* you are solving and *why* it matters.
*   **Validation:** Don't just run the code; verify it. For example, if you implement interpolation, plot the error against the real function to prove it works.
*   **Citations:** Cite your sources (including the course materials) to avoid plagiarism.
