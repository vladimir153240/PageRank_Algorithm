Based on the **Final Exam Project Guidelines**, here is a structured summary of the requirements, formats, and grading criteria to help guide your topic selection.

### **1. Core Objective & Philosophy**
*   **Goal:** The project is an opportunity to showcase your skills in mathematics and Python, applying critical thinking to a specific task.
*   **Approach:** You must follow the scientific method (or a similar structured approach) to create a complete project, moving from idea generation to submission.
*   **Originality:** The work **does not** need to be original or fill a research gap. You are encouraged to recreate already investigated problems, provided you do so with high standards and rigor.
*   **Guiding Principle:** Choose a topic you are proud of and would want to share with others.

### **2. Project Types & Topics**
You can mix and match approaches, but most projects fall into these categories:
*   **Algorithm Implementation & Analysis:** Implementing a math-heavy algorithm and analyzing its complexity or trade-offs.
*   **Math Explainer:** A deep-dive tutorial or explanation of a mathematical concept (often beginner-friendly).
*   **Math Applications:** Reviewing how math is applied in a specific field (e.g., music, radiology) with code examples.
*   **Games/Puzzles:** Creating simulations or proofs for math-based games, paradoxes, or brainteasers.
*   **Data Modeling:** Using datasets for analysis (though this is often less recommended compared to the others).

### **3. Mandatory Deliverables**
*   **Main File:** At least one Jupyter Notebook (`.ipynb`) containing your analysis, math, and Python code.
*   **Hosting:** A GitHub repository hosting your project.
*   **Commit Rule:** Your repository **must** have at least **10 meaningful commits on at least 10 different dates**. Failure to meet this results in a failing grade.

### **4. Grading Criteria**
The grading is flexible but generally follows this breakdown:
*   **Problem Formulation (0–15):** Clarity of the problem, scope, and objectives.
*   **Writing & Layout (0–20):** Structure, documentation quality, and clarity of explanations.
*   **Mathematical Understanding (0–15):** Correct usage and explanation of concepts, formulas, and references.
*   **Code Quality (0–15):** Readability, organization, documentation, and use of appropriate libraries.
*   **Methods & Data (0–20):** Correctness of implementation, validation of methods, and testing of assumptions.
*   **Communication (0–15):** Clear conclusions and overall presentation quality.

### **5. Essential Constraints**
*   **Deadline:** You must submit the link to your project before **16:00h on 17-March-2026**.
*   **Fail Conditions:** You will fail if you miss the deadline, cheat/plagiarize, break the law, submit non-functional code, or fail the "10 commits" rule.
